1Late 2018 — oversupply slide

Record US shale output plus trade-war demand fears cut crude ~40% in a quarter. Pump prices followed within weeks. In the spread view this period is calm — the gap barely moves when crude is the only thing changing.

2Q2 2020 — COVID demand crash

WTI averaged ~$28 and briefly went negative, yet gas only fell to ~$1.94. In the spread view this shows up as a spike, not a dip — crude collapsed faster than pump prices, so the gap temporarily widened.

32022 — Ukraine invasion spike

WTI hit a $108 quarterly average and gas set records. In real 2026 dollars this remains the decade's true peak — crude near $123 adjusted. The spread also blew out as refining capacity shortages let margins balloon.

42026 — Iran war, still unfolding

Crude is up ~49% since January after the Strait of Hormuz closure. In real dollars Q2 2026 crude (~$89) is still well below the 2022 peak — though the spread is widening again as refined products outrun crude.
